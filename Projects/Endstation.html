Endstation


“Endstation” is German for “Final stop”.

An escape-room is a physical adventure game in which players solve a series of puzzles using clues and hints within a time limit to ultimately escape it.

The setting for this escape-room is that the players witnessed a murder of a spy and got seen doing so. The murderer and his accomplice caught them and lock them in the spy's car, which is mounted on a train and set to arrive its final destination within an hour, where the witnesses can be disposed quietly this time.

Riddles

(4)


The original idea to build an escape room within a car meant its components should be part of our riddles. We created a setting in which two unfortunate bystanders witnessed the murder of a spy. Getting locked in his car with a bunch of spy gadgets made it plausible that there were hidden codes and an escape hatch; unlocking it before the car-carrying train arrived its final stop is the only way of getting out of this death trap alive. In order to unlock the door, the players will have to close three electric circuits. First, they need to find the ignition key, shift into reverse gear, use the hazard lights and press the horn. One player needs to hold the horn so that the second one can open the final bolt. Other riddles included cleaning the windshield so that the players can read a code behind it, and finding a window crank so that they can roll up a clue/number riddle.

Comments


It was a nice challenge to build an escape room in a very short period and with a tiny budget. Fun Fact: Edgar Schoenknecht coined the “no-budget-production” name, while solving problems the cheapest and quickest way imaginable. Our test-players, players and mentors were all very fond of what we accomplished. In fact, the praises were so high that it was even considered to show case it at some game conventions. However, our school lacked a parking lot nor was it prepared to fund it, thus we had to dismantle it heavy heartedly.

Team "Sind Augenverbinden und Fessel auch okay?"


The title came to be as we lacked a team name and just asked if it was okay to use blindfolds and shackles.

A group of Game-Design-Students from the Media-Design Hochschule (Berlin) assigned to build an escape room for the course level-design. Some focused on creating riddles, others installed them in the car, while others documented the process and recorded the play-throughs.
